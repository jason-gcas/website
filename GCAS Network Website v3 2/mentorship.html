The Philosophy

We Teach You To Think Like An Operator.

Consulting solves problems. Mentorship rewires how you think about the problems in the first place. Over thirty years, Jason Webb has mentored business owners from first contract to nine-figure enterprise. The patterns are knowable. The discipline is teachable. And the compounding effect — on revenue, on your team, on your family — is real.

"You can write somebody a winning proposal. That helps them win today. You can teach somebody to lead a company that wins every year for the rest of their career — and that changes the whole arc of a family. Mentorship is the work I care about most."
— Jason Webb, Founder & Managing Advisor

The Operator's Playbook

Six Disciplines We Teach Relentlessly.

Every mentorship relationship is individual, but the underlying curriculum is consistent. These are the six disciplines that separate a business that wins occasionally from an operation that wins predictably — and they are the organizing spine of our time together.

Discipline 01

Compliance As Competitive Moat

Treating safety, documentation, and certification not as cost centers but as the most defensible advantages in your competitive set.

Discipline 02

Pricing Discipline

Never bidding blind. Understanding what the incumbent charged, what the government expects, and where your win-probability inflection point lives.

Discipline 03

The Contracting Officer Relationship

Earning a contracting officer's advocacy is a repeatable strategy. We teach you the specific behaviors, communication cadence, and performance markers that unlock it.

Discipline 04

Scaling The Team Without Breaking It

Hiring sequences, org-chart evolution, compensation structures, and the three or four seats you hire before you ever think you need them.

Discipline 05

Capital & Cash Flow

Progress-payment structures, mobilization financing, surety bonding growth, and how to avoid the MCA-debt trap that kills otherwise-capable government contractors.

Discipline 06

The Exit You Actually Want

Whether your exit is a sale, a generational handoff, or an ESOP — we start shaping it from year one. The companies that exit well are the ones that planned the exit before they needed it.

The Arc

What A Year In Mentorship Looks Like.

Mentorship is a twelve-month minimum commitment because the compounding happens on a longer clock than a bid cycle. Here is the rhythm.

Months 1–2 — Assessment

Deep audit of business, leadership, team, financials, pipeline. We establish a 12-month roadmap with named outcomes.

Months 3–5 — Foundations

Install the operational disciplines — pricing rigor, CPAR posture, hiring playbook, compliance culture. This is where the habits take root.

Months 6–9 — Scale Moves

Larger bids. Teaming arrangements. Mentor-protégé candidates. Strategic hires. The period where the roadmap becomes visible revenue.

Months 10–12 — Compounding

Consolidate wins. Refresh pipeline. Begin the longer-horizon work: leadership succession, exit architecture, generational planning.
