Federal Contract Vehicles — Trained on Every One

Every structure.
Every acronym.

Consultants emerge fluent in the full federal contract vocabulary — because clients depend on them to translate.

IDIQ

Indefinite Delivery / Indefinite Quantity

Master contracts with no fixed quantity, used to issue task orders over multiple years. Once you're on an IDIQ, you compete only against other awardees for individual orders.

MATOC

Multiple Award Task Order Contract

A pool of pre-qualified contractors — typically construction or design — competing only among themselves for individual task orders. Heavily used by Army Corps and military commands.

SATOC

Single Award Task Order Contract

One contractor wins the master vehicle and receives all task orders without further competition. Hard to win, transformative when won.

NAVFAC

Naval Facilities Engineering Command

The Navy's primary construction and facilities procurement organization. Issues massive task orders through its own MATOC and SATOC structures across every U.S. naval installation.

SABER

Simplified Acquisition of Base Engineering Requirements

The Air Force's flagship base-construction MATOC vehicle. Streamlined task-order issuance for facilities work on USAF installations.

JOC

Job Order Contract

An IDIQ-style vehicle using a unit-priced book (often RSMeans) to issue rapid task orders for repair, alteration, and minor construction. Heavily used by federal agencies and state DGS systems.

IFB

Invitation for Bid

Sealed-bid competition. The agency awards strictly to the lowest-priced, technically responsive bidder. The compliance bar is the highest of any vehicle — one missed line item and you're out.

RFP

Request for Proposal

Best-value competition. The agency evaluates technical approach, past performance, key personnel, and price. Margin is defensible. Capabilities statement quality is decisive.

RFQ

Request for Quote

Used in simplified acquisitions (under the SAT) and for GSA Schedule orders. Faster cycle, lighter documentation, but still requires full compliance to even be considered.

BPA

Blanket Purchase Agreement

A standing arrangement with an agency for recurring work — especially under GSA Schedules. A BPA holder is on the inside; an outsider trying to win the same work has to fight uphill.

GWAC

Government-Wide Acquisition Contract

Federal-wide IDIQs (Alliant, OASIS+, 8(a) STARS, etc.) used by any agency. Highly competitive to get on, but a single award puts you in the running for thousands of task orders.

GSA

GSA Schedule (MAS)

The Multiple Award Schedule — the largest commercial procurement vehicle in the federal government. A GSA Schedule contract is a 20-year hunting license for ordering activities across all agencies.

SubNet

SBA Subcontracting Network

The largest federal subcontracting opportunity portal in the world. Most small businesses do not even know it exists. Trained consultants register clients and unlock a separate, less-competitive opportunity stream.

8(a)

8(a) Sole-Source & Set-Aside

The 8(a) program permits sole-source awards up to $7M (manufacturing) and $4.5M (services). Sole-source means no competition. A trained consultant identifies, qualifies, and pursues these.

SAT

Simplified Acquisition Threshold

Procurements under $250K (currently) follow streamlined rules and are mandatorily reserved for small businesses between the micro-purchase threshold and the SAT. A high-volume, low-friction lane for trained consultants.

This is the working vocabulary every GCAS consultant is fluent in by the end of training. Beyond the acronyms is the operational fluency: when each vehicle applies, who awards it, how to register, and how to win.

The Hidden Market

Opportunities the
public never sees.

SAM.gov is the visible iceberg. A trained GCAS consultant has access to procurement portals, agency networks, and subcontracting boards that are invisible to a typical small-business owner — and often invisible to consultants who haven't been trained on them.

Agency-Direct Portals

DLA, USACE, Navy Electronic Commerce Online, NASA SEWP, VA's VECTOR — every major agency runs procurement systems beyond SAM.gov. A consultant registers clients on each.

SubNet & Subcontracting

Every federal prime contract over $700K requires a small-business subcontracting plan. SubNet is where those subcontract opportunities post — and where most small businesses never look.

State & Municipal Boards

All 50 states, thousands of cities, school districts, transit authorities, and utility districts run their own procurement portals — many with explicit small-business set-asides. A consultant maps and monitors.
